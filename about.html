The Author




Herb Hughes worked in the high tech industry for more than two decades before leaving to start his own business. This new endeavor was built from the ground up, where he turned something that had not existed before into a household name in the locality. Once the business was thriving, he sold it to pursue his lifelong dream of writing novels.

The authorâ€™s previous publications include four nonfiction books and numerous articles and short stories in national and regional periodicals. But the novels were always there, churning in the background, waiting for their chance to spring forward.


His first two novels, Tennessee Yankee and The Joystick Murders, representing many years of work, were released together. The novels are different in genre, time frame, subject, and writing style. The author says:


"I enjoy studying the past and reading historical novels, but I also look to the future and read science fiction. My present is mostly working at a laptop and playing the occasional semi-pathetic round of golf. Who wants to read about that? So I write in two genres, the past and the future: historical fiction and science fiction/future fiction."
